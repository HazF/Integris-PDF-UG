Checking for Implied PIM

Passive Intermodulation (PIM) distortion can be caused by 'imperfections' in mechanical components of the Radiated Frequency (RF) path to and from the installed RRH, but also by some metallic structures in the immediate vicinity of the radio head.

Examples of causes can include faults in connectors and cables, damage to the antenna itself, or even tower handrails.

PIM is detected by looking for its effects on the power spectrum. PIM typically exhibits a 'Shark Fin' graph (See Fig 1.1 ) caused by the interaction of spurious signals generated by the fault. As well as distorting the signal, PIM can raise the 'noise floor', obliterating weaker incoming signals and impairing receiver sensitivity.
